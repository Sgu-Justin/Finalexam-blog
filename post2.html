Nutrition rules that will fuel your workout

What you eat, and when, has a big impact on your energy level and how well you recover from a workout.

Posted by Justin Sye on August 24, 2023

Rule #1: Pay attention

The significance of nutrition basics is often underestimated by many active adults, leading to a shortfall in essential nutrients. Inadequate intake of vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients can jeopardize both health and performance. Adhering to the principles of a healthy diet—abundant in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, healthy fats, whole-grain carbohydrates, and sufficient fluids, particularly water—serves as a fundamental guideline for fueling up for physical activities.

Rule #2: Fuel up

Providing your body with the necessary energy is crucial, even if weight loss is the objective. Skimping on nutrition can result in muscle loss, reduced bone density, and fatigue, posing risks of injury, illness, prolonged recovery, hormonal imbalances, and menstrual issues for women. Ensuring an adequate intake of nutrient-dense calories supports both exercise and overall health.

Rule #3: Love carbs (you need them)

Carbohydrates, often misunderstood, play a pivotal role in sustaining long and high-intensity exercises. Research spanning the last 50 years emphasizes the necessity of carbs, especially for active individuals. While some trends promote high-fat, low-carb diets, evidence suggests they hinder rather than boost athletic performance. Carbs serve as vital fuel for the brain and muscles during workouts, and the recommended intake varies based on exercise intensity and duration.

Rule #4: Rebuild with protein

Protein is indispensable for its role in building and repairing muscles, providing essential amino acids. Research recommends a protein intake of 1.2 to 2 grams per kilogram of body weight for very active individuals. Good protein sources include poultry, fish, soybeans, legumes, eggs, Greek yogurt, cheese, and tofu.

Rule #5: Don't ignore fats

Despite confusion surrounding fats, they are essential for a healthy diet, offering energy and aiding in the absorption of vitamins. Opt for unsaturated fats found in avocado, olive and canola oils, flaxseed, and nuts.

Rule #6: Know what you need pre-workout

For workouts lasting less than an hour, eating throughout the day should suffice for energy needs. However, it's advisable to avoid eating immediately before exercising to prevent gastrointestinal issues. Generally, consume a meal one to three hours before a workout, even for sustained, high-intensity activities like a half marathon.

Rule #7: Remember post-workout 15

After utilizing stored energy during a workout, prompt nutrient restoration is essential. Research suggests consuming protein-rich foods within 15 minutes post-workout to provide essential amino acids for muscle building and repair. Replenishing carbohydrates and fluids is equally vital, and a post-workout smoothie can be an effective strategy for recovery.
